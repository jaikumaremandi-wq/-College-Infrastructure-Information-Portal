                          College Infrastructure Information Portal
The rapid expansion of educational institutions has led to a reliance on manual methods for managing and sharing information regarding campus infrastructure, often resulting in inefficient information retrieval for students, faculty, and visitors. The College Infrastructure Information Portal is a web-based application designed to bridge this gap by providing a centralized, digital platform that consolidates essential data on campus facilities, including buildings, laboratories, libraries, and hostels. The research design follows a system development life cycle approach, utilizing frontend technologies (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) for an intuitive interface and backend technology (PHP/MySQL) for secure data management. The portal allows administrators to update facility details and users to navigate the campus effectively through interactive tools. Major findings indicate that the developed portal successfully digitizes facility management, reducing the time spent on manual queries and improving the overall user experience for campus navigation and resource location. The study concludes that an automated, web-based infrastructure portal enhances operational efficiency and acts as a vital tool for modern, information-driven educational environments.

College infrastructure includes physical and digital resources essential for education, featuring modern classrooms, specialized laboratories, well-stocked libraries, and computing facilities. Key amenities include hostels, cafeterias, sports complexes, Wi-Fi, and medical facilities, with many institutions focusing on ICT-enabled learning tools like smartboards, projectors, and internet-connected workstations to support all-round student development. 

Key Components of College Infrastructure:
•	Academic Facilities: Spacious lecture halls, specialized research laboratories, computer centers, and libraries with e-resources like INFLIBNET.
•	Residential & Recreational: Hostels with single/quad occupancy, dining messes, cafeterias, gymnasiums, and sports complexes.
•	Digital Infrastructure: Campus-wide Wi-Fi, LAN-connected computers, 24/7 internet, and ICT-enabled classrooms with smartboards.
•	Maintenance: Structured systems for managing facilities such as benches, lighting, and air conditioning to maintain an effective learning environment. 
Typical Infrastructure Standards (As per regulatory guidelines):
•	Land Requirements: Minimum 5 acres in metropolitan areas, 10 acres in non-urban areas.
•	Building Space: Dedicated administrative blocks, academic wings (approx. 10,000 sq. meters), and specialized labs.
•	Resource Capacity: Adequate seating capacity in messes (e.g., 300+ students) and proper safety facilities.
